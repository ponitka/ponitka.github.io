About Me

I am a PhD student at Tel Aviv University 🇮🇱 advised by Michal Feldman. During my PhD studies, I spent a year at Sapienza University of Rome 🇮🇹 hosted by Stefano Leonardi and a month at Stanford University 🇺🇸 hosted by Aviad Rubinstein. I completed my undergraduate studies at the University of Oxford 🇬🇧 where I was advised by Elias Koutsoupias. I attended the 14th High School in Wrocław 🇵🇱 where I was active in competitive programming.

My name is Tomasz, where the 'sz' is pronounced like the 'sh' in English. I also go by Tomek.

Research Interests

I work on problems at the intersection of computer science and economics. My research field is known as Economics and Computation, or Algorithmic Game Theory.

Most of my work focuses on Contract Design and Fair Division.

Find my work on: Google Scholar DBLP

I co-organized a tutorial on Algorithmic Contract Design for Teams at EC 2026.
